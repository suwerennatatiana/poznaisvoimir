About the Series

“Know Your World — Look Into Your Memory” is a growing educational book series created to help children gently remember their connection with Earth, nature, water, seasons, animals — and with their own inner world.

The books speak with children sincerely, without fear or pressure — through stories, images, and simple truths about how the world works and how we can live in harmony with it.

Why These Books Matter

Many children today live among screens — while losing their natural bond with Earth. This series restores that connection in a calm, thoughtful way.

  • teaches how the Earth really works, in honest simple language
  • awakens curiosity and respect for nature
  • helps children feel confident and safe
  • shows that every person is a creator, not just a consumer
  • gives practical knowledge useful in real life

These books help children grow kinder, more mindful and aware — without losing joy, imagination or freedom.

Existing and Planned Books in the Series

Mother Earth
A journey through our living planet — soil, trees, forests, mountains, roots, insects and seasons — learning that the Earth is alive and needs our care.

The Story of Water
Rivers, oceans, clouds and rain — where water comes from, why it is sacred, and how children can help protect it.

The Four Directions
North, South, East and West explained through games, observation and movement — developing a natural sense of space and navigation.

The Earth’s Magnetic Field
An invisible shield that protects our planet — told simply through animals, nature, compasses and real examples.

Finding Water in Heat
Safe, gentle survival knowledge — where water can be found in nature, why preparation matters, and how to stay respectful to the environment.

Humans as Creators
How thoughts, choices and actions shape our world — and how every child can help nature through kindness, creativity and responsibility.

Helping Nature Through Our Actions
Simple everyday things a child can do: planting, protecting, reusing, cleaning, sharing and bringing harmony instead of harm.

Each book reminds children: they are part of Earth, part of life — and their presence truly matters.
